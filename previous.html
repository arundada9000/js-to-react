Important Teaching Rules

8 principles for success

Teaching someone coding is honestly one of the sweetest things ever. Do not try to make her "memorize JavaScript". Your goal is to make her comfortable, curious, confident, and excited to build things.

Never rush Make her type everything Let her make mistakes Laugh at bugs together Build tiny projects often Teach visually Explain WHY before HOW Repeat concepts naturally

Best Learning Method

The fastest way to learn
Step 1: You explain the concept.
Step 2: You code one example.
Step 3: She explains it back to you.
Step 4: She builds a similar thing herself.

This is proven to be the fastest learning method for programming.

Important Teaching Advice

When she gets stuck & how to keep her motivated
When she gets stuck: Never immediately give the answer. Instead ask: "What is the error? What do you think is happening? Which line looks suspicious?" This develops problem-solving skills.

How to Keep Her Motivated

Celebrate tiny wins "That was real developer thinking!" Build things she personally likes Do coding dates "1 bug challenge" together Let her decorate projects

Biggest Beginner Mistakes

Watching too many tutorials, not coding enough, memorizing syntax, fearing errors, comparing themselves. Errors are NORMAL. Every developer Googles things.

Final Goal

What success looks like

By the end she should be able to:

Think logically Build projects Debug code Search solutions Understand frontend Understand backend Connect database Create full MERN apps

And most importantly: enjoy coding.
